Group Publication

Black holes are bizarre spacetime structure and astronomical objects which play an important role at different scales in the Universe. Black Hole Astrophysics Group at the Department of Physics, National Taiwan Normal University aim to investigate astronomical black holes systems and their observable relativistic properties through theoretical understandings, with collaborations worldwide.

We are looking for motivated student and postdocs! You are more than welcome to contact and chat with Hung-Yi (hypu at ntnu.phys.edu.tw) if you are a graduate or undergraduate who is interested in (or not sure about) these fields!

Research Topics

Black Hole Magnetosphere, Accretion, Jet

Gravity and Magnetic field both play an crucial role shaping the black hole accretion-jet system. General Relativisitic Magnetopydrodynamics (GRMHD) is therefore an essential tool to explore the interesting properties in the vicinity of black holes.

example: GRMHD Simulation of Potential M87 Accretion Disk (by Illinois group)

Black Hole Shadow and Emission Features

Radiative transfer in curved spacetime can bring rich information about the black hole and its environment, providing valuable tests of strong gravity and accretion/jet theory. We are supporting Event Horizon Telescope (EHT) and Greenland Telescope (GLT) projects with theoretical studies, interoperation, and analysis.

Event Horizon Telescope website

Gravitational Wave

Extreme Mass Ratio Inspiral systems which consisting of one massive black hole and one stellar-mass black hole are promising and interesting sources to be explored in the upcoming Laser Interferometer Space Antenna (LISA) epoch.
